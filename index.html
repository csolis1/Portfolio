About Me

I earned a Bachelors in International Affairs with a concentration in diplomatic service at Kennesaw State University with the strong desire to work in areas where tech and politics intersect.

I graduated cum laude and was a member of many organizations during the 4 years of my academic career including Model United Nations, the International Student Association, Kennesaw Women’s Rugby and the Latin American Association. During my college career, I studied both traditional Political Science and interdisciplinary fields such as Global Security, Study of international Terrorism, Political Philosophy, Criminal Justice, Comparative politics, Foreign policy with focus on Latin American, European and African Politics.

Outside of college, I developed a deeper appreciation of various quantitative fields of study, honing my skills in data visualization, statistical analysis and various programming languages. My journey into tech started by earning a full Nanodegree scholarship in the Bertelsmann Data Science Scholarship offered by Udacity. I was blessed to be able to work with Goodie Nation, a non-profit tech focused organization that serves underrepresented businesses and entrepreneurs. Partnering with City of Refuge's Tech Academy, we assisted in a Hack-a-thon that exposed students to valuable experience and connected them to seasoned developers. Currently, I am in the process of receiving a professional certificate as a fullstack developer with Digital Craft's web development bootcamp. Through my experience in various programming languages and frameworks including Python, SQL, Javascript, JQuery, CSS, HTML, Node.js, React and Express, I found my passion for tech galvanized with a strong desire to continue creating and surround myself with people that share my resolve. My interests include mixed martial arts, rugby and art. I have had the honor being part of various art based organizations and initiatives in Atlanta including Contrapunto, a Latin American art collective that celebrates and promotes Latin art. I humbly accept how little of this world I have experienced and it continues to fuels my desire to learn more.

My goal is to be able to immerse myself in and contribute to projects based in science and innovation to solve issues down to the individual level.
